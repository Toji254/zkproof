# Competitive Positioning

## Why this wins

Most privacy projects in this field are either:
1. crypto-native payment/privacy infrastructure, or
2. institutional compliance / solvency tools.

ProofPass is different: it solves a normal human problem in plain English.
A renter needs to prove they qualify.
A landlord needs a yes/no answer.
Neither side needs to understand crypto.

## Why this is not just proof-of-funds

ProofPass should not be pitched as a generic wallet-balance checker.
The winning framing is private qualification for real life.

Primary hero use case:
- rental pre-qualification

Secondary follow-on use cases:
- visa proof-of-funds
- scholarship eligibility
- freelancer trust checks
- loan pre-checks

This makes the product broader and more human than a pure proof-of-funds demo.

## How to beat zkCred

zkCred is the closest direct competitor because it also uses private threshold proofs around financial eligibility.

ProofPass should beat it on:
- clearer consumer story
- stronger renter / landlord narrative
- simpler verifier UX
- explicit failure-case demo
- better judge quickstart packaging

The message should be:
- zkCred proves funds
- ProofPass proves qualification

## How to beat institutional solvency projects

Projects like Auspex are strong because they are polished and technically serious.
They are also more institutional and less universal.

ProofPass wins when it says:
- this is not a treasury or auditor tool
- this is for normal people
- landlords, schools, lenders, and service providers can use it immediately

## Why rental qualification is the best first story

Rental qualification is:
- instantly understandable
- emotionally real
- easy to demo in under 90 seconds
- relevant outside crypto

A judge should understand the product in one sentence:

"This lets a renter prove they qualify without exposing their financial life."
